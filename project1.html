About Real Estate Chennai
CHENNAI
An Overview
Chennai is a Metropolitan city of state Tamil Nadu and the capital city of Tamil Nadu. Chennai was earlier known as Madras till 1996. Chennai lies off the Bay of Bengal on the Coromandel coast. Chennai is the Biggest Cultural Capital of South India. Chennai is the sixth most populated city in India as of 2011. Chennai was one of the most visited places by the tourists and was ranked 43rd in the list of the most visited city in the world in 2015. It is known as Indias Health capital. In 2014, Chennai was ranked as the best city in India and in 2015, it was named as the hottest city. It is the 9th best cosmopolitan city in the world. Chennai is having one-third of Indias automobile industry and hence it is called Detroit of India. It is also a home for Tamil Film Industry.
History
Madras(Chennai) was found on 22nd August 1639 by the East Indian Company. In 1996, the Government of Tamil Nadu officially changed the name from Madras to Chennai. The name Chennai is derived from the name Chennaipattanam, a town near Fort St.George. Chennai is popular for IT and industrial hub and also famous for music. There was a village called Madrasipatnam from where the word Madras was derived. After the independence in 1947, Chennai became the capital of Madras state which was later renamed as Tamil Nadu in 1969. The official language of Chennai is Tamil. English, Malayalam, Telugu and Urdu are also spoken.
Economy, Industry and Employment
The economy of Chennai is ranked as the sixth most productive metro area in India. Chennai is a base for large scale industries like automobiles, technology, computers etc. Chennai was the second largest exporter of BPO sectors and Information Technology(IT). It is ranked as the third highest by GDP per capita. Some of the automobile industries are

Renault
Robert Bosch
Ashok Leyland
Royal Enfield
BharatBenz
Chennai is a home for three major banks. They are National Level Commercial Banks, Indian Bank and Indian Overseas Bank. Chennai is also called as the Banking Capital of India. The first ever Bank in Chennai is The Madras Bank. Madras Stock Exchange deals with all the stock exchange in the city. In South Asia, Chennai is emerging as an electronic manufacturing hub with many companies like Samsung, Cisco, Siemens, Dell etc.

Demographics
The total population of Chennai according to 2011 census is 4,681,087. The average literacy rate of Chennai city is 90.18 per cent of which male and female literacy was 93.70 and 86.64 per cent. The sex ratio of Chennai city is 989 females per 1000 males. Children from 9.88% of the total population of Chennai City.

Connectivity
Roads
Chennai is connected with many cities through four major National Highways i.e., connected by the Golden Quadrilateral system. They are

NH4 - to Mumbai
NH5 - to Kolkata
NH45 - to Theni
NH205 - to Madanapalle
Bus - Chennai Mofussil Bus Terminus (CMBT) was one of the largest bus stations in Asia and it is the main bus station in Chennai that connects many cities and states. The Volvo buses are operated by the Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (TNSTC).
Rail
Chennai is the headquarters for Southern Railways. There are four major railway terminals. They are

Chennai Central
Chennai Egmore
Chennai Beach
Tambaram
Chennai Central is the main terminal for the city that connects with many cities all over India. Chennai metro was started on 29th June 2015. The metro consists of two lines, Blue Lines & Green Line that connects around 40 stations covering a distance of 45.1km. The Blue Line connects to the Chennai International Airport.

Air
Chennai International Airport, located in Meenambakkam about 21km away from the city. The Chennai Airport is the fourth busiest airport in India. It was initially called Meenambakkam Airport when it was operated in the year 1985 only for Domestic flights. Later when International flights started to operate, it was renamed as Chennai International Airport.
